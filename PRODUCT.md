# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** 16–29 year olds who care about music, gaming, and culture but haven't bought serious audio gear before. They are city and online-native, hyper-online, and include "performative males" and "cool offline people" who want gear that signals taste without requiring deep technical knowledge.

**Contexts:**

- Discovering better sound for the first time after using phone buds, AirPods, or gaming headsets.
- Buying gear that fits a personal aesthetic: streetwear, warehouse-rave, skate, and minimal-lux references.
- Comparing IEMs by sound signature, use case, and price without wading into audiophile forum jargon.
- Shopping for bundles (IEM + DAC) to level up gaming performance or music listening on a budget.

**Job to be done:** Find affordable, good-looking IEMs and related gear that sound better than what they use now, presented in a way that feels like a brand they actually want to wear and share.

## Product Purpose

Plugged Audio is a curated IEM reseller proving that affordable audio gear doesn't have to suck. The store makes first-time IEM buying feel less like a technical chore and more like joining a culture: better sound, honest curation, no gatekeeping.

Success looks like a visitor understanding why an IEM is worth buying, trusting the curation, and completing a purchase — or coming back because the site feels like a place, not a catalog.

## Brand Personality

- **Educational but unpretentious:** Explain sound signatures, DACs, and fit without talking down or flexing expertise.
- **Raw but premium:** Underground texture and brutalist energy, but the product photography, type, and interactions feel considered.
- **Confident but welcoming:** The tone assumes the visitor deserves better gear, not that they need to earn it.

**Three-word personality:** raw, honest, plugged-in.

## Anti-references

- No sterile audiophile forum or reviewer-site energy (headphones.com, hangout.audio, linsoul, aliexpress).
- No generic electronics marketplace that lists headphones as an afterthought category.
- No Amazon or eBay visual language: star ratings everywhere, aggressive discount badges, cluttered grids.
- No basic "white-label" ecommerce templates with no point of view.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the experience, not the wrapper.** The interface should feel like walking into a curated record store or warehouse pop-up, not browsing a spreadsheet of specs.
2. **Specs serve the story.** Technical details (drivers, impedance, sound signature, frequency response) appear when they help a decision, never as the lead.
3. **Sound is the product, but taste is the hook.** Every product is framed by how it fits a listener, gamer, or scene — not just how it measures.
4. **Curation over infinite choice.** A small, edited collection beats an overwhelming catalog. Empty states and "why this exists" copy matter.
5. **Affordable doesn't look cheap.** Pricing is honest and accessible, but the presentation — photography, typography, motion, packaging cues — feels premium and intentional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: no layout-shifting motion, no auto-playing media, no essential information conveyed by animation alone.
- Maintain strong color contrast even with textured backgrounds; grunge must not interfere with text legibility.
- Use clear, plain language for audio concepts so first-time buyers aren't excluded by jargon.
